In Pieces of Eight by Steve Goble, the author explores the theme of the consequences of one's actions, voluntary or involuntary, through the use of foreshadowing. Through the story of Spider John, the protagonist, the author showcases how the choices and events in his life have led to significant outcomes.

It highlights the theme of the text that your actions, whether voluntary or involuntary, have major consequences, as Spider is about to experience the consequences of leaving his family for nearly a decade. Through the use of foreshadowing, Steve Goble effectively conveys the central idea that your actions, whether voluntary or involuntary, have major consequences. He uses this writing strategy to create suspense and uncertainty in the reader, and to build a deeper understanding of the protagonist's experiences and the impact of his choices. Pieces of Eight is a historical adventure that explores this theme in an engaging and thought-provoking way, leaving the reader with much to reflect upon long after the story ends.
